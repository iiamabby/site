Āheinga

There are many ways for you to upskill and build the foundation for an amazing career in Open Source.

Check out the links below to learn more, access a range of great training opportunities and to connect with your local and global tech communities.

Mentorships & Internships

Google Summer of Code

An international annual program in which Google awards stipends to contributors who successfully complete a free and open-source software coding project during the summer.

LFX Mentorship

Get involved in the Linux Foundation’s leading mentorship programme, designed to help train the next generation of open source developers, supported through sponsorship and specialised industry training.

(Currently being adapted for participants in the Southern Hemisphere).

Outreachy

Outreachy provides internships in open source and open science to people subject to systemic bias and impacted by underrepresentation in the technical industry where they are living.

Training & Development

KCNA Essentials Course

This course provides an overview of cloud native technologies and how container orchestration systems like Kubernetes can help to implement and maintain them.

*Ask about sponsored access for Māori cohorts

Kubernetes Cloud Native Associate (Exam)

The KCNA exam demonstrates a user’s foundational knowledge and skills in Kubernetes and the wider cloud native ecosystem.

*Ask about sponsored access for Māori cohorts

EDX Essentials Course

Want to learn Kubernetes for free? Get an in-depth primer on this powerful system for managing containerised applications.

Community Engagement

Slack

Join the Kubernetes and CNCF channels on Slack to take your first step into global open source communities to begin your Contributor journey!

Kubernetes Contributor Guide

Kubernetes is a sophisticated skillset that can involve a deep learning curve to master, but thankfully there’s an entire community and wide range of roles you can undertake to become an active Contributor. Learn more here!

Cloud Native Meetup (BOP)

Connect with like-minded locals in the Bay of Plenty tech community to explore evolving technology, get support with careers and training, and build your professional networks.

Need support or more information? Contact our team directly.
