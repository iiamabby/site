Our Mission

Te Whainga

Technology continues to influence the world of work.

This means immense opportunity for future careers and learning, but also an increased need to ensure our people are well-equipped to get involved.

We believe to effectively support growth opportunities in the tech sector, it needs a community-centric approach that bridges transitional gaps between individuals, education, industry and government, while upholding the hauora (wellbeing) of potential Contributors.

'Kupenga' is a pilot Te Ao Māori mentoring initiative we're developing to make it easier for our people to contribute to Open Source and position Aotearoa in the future of Cloud.

Our Values

​Whanonga pono

The New Zealand Government estimates that up to one in five New Zealanders experience some form of digital exclusion due to a lack of access, affordability, skills, trust, or the motivation to get online - Salmon, Kaleah. 2023

Through our initiatives, we aim to:

Empower underepresented groups:

  • We are passionate about increasing the representation of Māori and females in the tech space. By providing support, mentorship, and opportunities, we seek to bridge the diversity gap and empower underrepresented individuals to thrive in tech careers.

Foster Collaboration

  • We believe that collaboration is key to unlocking the full potential of technology. Our community serves as a hub for like-minded individuals to connect, share knowledge, and work together on cloud-native, open-source projects that push the boundaries of innovation.

Promote open source principles

  • Open source lies at the heart of our community. We advocate for the free exchange of ideas and knowledge, enabling continuous learning and collective progress in the tech industry.

Our Community

Te Hāpori

Nāu te rourou, nāku te rourou, ka ora ai te iwi
"With your contributions and my contributions, the community thrives"

  • 2022
    Canvas Career Expo
    The team reintroduced Open Source at the regions largest annual career expo, using eight Raspberry Pi-powered monitors to showcase its capabilities and learning opportunities. Attendees from local schools, businesses, and community organizations learned about Linux, mentorship programs like LFX and Google Summer of Code, Raspberry Pi, and the transformative potential of open source in career and education.
  • 2023
    Rotorua Career Expo
    ii alongside Toi Kai Rawa promoted opportunuitys for Maori economic development and leadership, as well as demonstrating how Minecraft can be used as a tool to learn how to program, promoting open source and transparency.
  • May - 2023
    Te Puke Science Day
    ii alongside Toi Kai Rawa attended the Science Day at Tepuke high school, the event was to encourage rangatahi to choose science and digital literacy as their topics next year, using Minecraft the attendees viewed first hand how code can be contributed to various projects.
  • August - 2023
    Canvas Career Expo
    ii worked alongside Toi Kai Rawa to promote digital technology career pathways and opportunities, using Minecraft java the attendees where encouraged to build upon the work of others in an open source Minecraft World, Demonstrating the collaborative nature of the open source community
  • August - 2023
    KCD Sydney
    Our community manager Jay Tihema was requested to speak at this years KCD Sydney, broading minds to the importance of mentorship, transparency and diversity in the open source / CNCF community.
